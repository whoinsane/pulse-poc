Tier A: Critical Fixes (Mandatory)
These MUST be addressed for the script to be market-ready and package-able.
A1: Strengthen Act I Emotional Foundation
The Problem
  • Audience doesn't empathize with Vikram early
  • No family stakes visible in Act I
  • Vikram presented as functional, not vulnerable
  • Weak engagement for first 20-25 minutes
The Fix
  • Introduce Isha/Akhil via photo, voicemail, or memory
  • Show Vikram's guilt/shame visually (not told)
  • Add subtle addiction struggle hints
  • Remove tone-conflicting bar elements
  • Give Vikram one "save the cat" moment early
Implementation: Rewrite first 12-15 pages. Add 1-2 emotional beats. Remove 1 comedic beat. Total impact: Transforms early engagement.
A2: Reduce Ranjit's Overlong Monologues
The Problem
  • Thriller pacing slows during speeches
  • Psychological menace diluted by over-explanation
  • Monologues over 15 lines lose impact
  • Dialogue carries weight action should show
The Fix
  • Trim speeches by 20-40%
  • Increase strategic silence
  • Use nonverbal behavior replacing exposition
  • Let actions demonstrate philosophy
  • Keep strongest lines, cut redundancy
Implementation: Pass through scenes 18-60. Trim 6-8 major speeches. Add silence beats. Pacing improves immediately.
A3: Foreshadow Identity-Switch Plan Earlier
The Problem
  • Twist arrives too late without setup
  • Lacks narrative foreshadowing
  • Revelation feels sudden, not earned
  • Misses opportunities for dread buildup
The Fix
  • Ranjit observing Vikram's height/body marks
  • Oddly detailed questions about Vikram's life
  • Comments about "fitting" or "replacing"
  • Subtle wardrobe sizing moments
  • Burn references planted earlier
Implementation: Add 3-4 micro-beats in Act II-A (pages 30-60). Each 1-2 lines. Builds latent dread, makes reveal satisfying.
A4: Eliminate Tonal Inconsistencies
The Problem
  • Unintentional humor breaks dread
  • Roopa's scenes veer toward comedy
  • Tonal whiplash reduces tension
  • Genre identity becomes muddy
The Fix
  • Remove comedic beats entirely
  • Play Roopa as intuitive, not naive
  • Direct interactions with subtext of danger
  • Maintain dark thriller tone throughout
  • Keep innocence, remove slapstick
Implementation: Adjust Roopa's 4-5 key scenes. Remove 2-3 comic beats. Reframe interactions as ominous, not funny.
A5: Clarify Police Station Geography
The Problem
  • Fire sequence lacks spatial clarity
  • Body hiding moments confusing
  • Exit paths unclear
  • Skylight/yard relationship vague
The Fix
  • Add early "walk and talk" establishing geography
  • Adjust blocking descriptions
  • Clean exit path references
  • Clarify skylight/courtyard relation
  • Add simple floor plan note in script
Implementation: Add 1 scene early (page 15-20) establishing layout. Adjust 3-4 action descriptions in Act III. Spatial clarity crucial for fire climax.
